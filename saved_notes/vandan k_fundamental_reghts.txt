 STUDY NOTES FOR FOUNDATIONAL REIGHTS

1. Definition
- Fundamental rights are the basic human rights that every person is entitled to by virtue of being a human being, regardless of their nationality, race, gender, or any other status. These rights protect individual freedoms and dignity.

2. Key Points 
- Individual freedom - The ability to make choices and express oneself without interference.
- Equality before the law - Everyone should be treated equally under the law.
- Due process of law - Fair treatment and legal procedures must be followed when dealing with individuals.
- Right to life, liberty, and security of person - Every person has the right to live freely, make choices, and be safe from harm.

3. Important Terms
- Fundamental rights: Basic human rights that are fundamental to a free and just society.
- Discrimination: The unfair or prejudicial treatment of individuals based on characteristics such as race, gender, religion, etc.
- Equality: Fair and impartial treatment for all individuals, regardless of their differences.
- Due process: Following proper legal procedures and fair treatment in legal matters.

4. Examples
- Fundamental rights are protected by various international and national laws, such as the Universal Declaration of Human Rights and the Constitution.
- Discrimination can take many forms, including racial discrimination, gender-based discrimination, or discrimination based on disability.
- Equality is an essential principle that ensures fairness in areas like employment, education, and access to public services.
- Due process guarantees individuals their right to a fair trial and protection from arbitrary arrests or detentions.

5. Short Summary
Fundamental rights are basic human rights that protect individual freedoms, equality, and due process. They include the right to freedom of speech, religion, and assembly. It is important to recognize and respect these rights to ensure a just and inclusive society for all.
